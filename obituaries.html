Raymond Earl Combs
February 14, 1946 — April 3, 2025  ·  Age 79  ·  Harlan, KY
Raymond Earl Combs, 79, of Harlan passed away Thursday, April 3, 2025, at his home surrounded by his family. Born February 14, 1946, in Harlan County, he was the son of the late Curtis and Mabel (Howard) Combs.

Raymond retired after 31 years of service with the Harlan County Road Department, where he was known to his colleagues as a dependable and meticulous worker who took visible pride in every mile of road he maintained. In his retirement years, he spent his time gardening, fishing Martins Fork with his grandchildren, and attending services at Calvary Baptist Church, where he was a faithful member for over fifty years.

He is survived by his wife of 54 years, Carolyn Sue (Baker) Combs; his son Terry Combs and wife Angela of Harlan; his daughter Lisa Combs Napier and husband David of Evarts; and five grandchildren: Kayla, Bryce, Addison, Hunter, and Cole. He was preceded in death by his parents, his brother Gary Combs, and his sister Paulette Gibson.

Funeral services were held Saturday, April 5, at Calvary Baptist Church, with burial at Resthaven Memorial Park. In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations to the Harlan County Animal Shelter.

Dorothy Mae Hensley
September 3, 1940 — April 1, 2025  ·  Age 84  ·  Cumberland, KY
Dorothy Mae Hensley, 84, of Cumberland, passed peacefully on the morning of April 1, 2025, at Harlan ARH Regional Medical Center. She was born September 3, 1940, to the late Orville and Edna (Saylor) Brock in Letcher County.

Dorothy was a retired school secretary who spent 27 years at Cumberland Elementary School, where generations of students and parents knew her as the calm, capable center of the building's daily life. She was a gifted quilter whose work was twice featured in the Kentucky State Fair's juried textile exhibition, and she was an enthusiastic and brutally competitive participant in her Tuesday afternoon bridge club well into her early eighties.

She is survived by her three children: Ronald Hensley (Kathleen) of Middlesboro, Sandra Hensley Floyd (Mark) of Cumberland, and Thomas Hensley of Lexington; eight grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren. She was preceded in death by her husband of 49 years, Gerald Wayne Hensley, and her sister Maxine Bowling.

A memorial service will be held Saturday, April 12, at 2:00 p.m. at the Cumberland First United Methodist Church. The family will receive friends one hour prior to the service. A graveside committal will follow at Cumberland Memorial Gardens.

Walter "Hoot" Gibson Jr.
March 22, 1958 — March 30, 2025  ·  Age 66  ·  Evarts, KY
Walter "Hoot" Gibson Jr., 66, of Evarts, passed away Sunday, March 30, 2025. Born March 22, 1958, he was the son of the late Walter Gibson Sr. and June (Combs) Gibson.

Hoot worked for many years in the underground coal mines of Harlan County before a back injury led him to transition to equipment maintenance, a trade he pursued until his retirement in 2019. He was a fixture at Evarts High School football games for four decades and coached youth baseball for eleven seasons through the Evarts Recreation League. Those who knew him will remember his laugh, his devotion to his family, and his deeply held conviction that no problem in life could not be at least partially addressed by a good meal and a longer story.

He is survived by his wife Debra Ann (Lawson) Gibson; his children Jessica Gibson Miracle of Harlan and Ryan Gibson of Evarts; and four grandchildren. He was preceded in death by his parents and his brother Kenneth Gibson.

Visitation was held Thursday, April 3, at Collins Funeral Home in Evarts. Burial followed at Resthaven Memorial Park.
