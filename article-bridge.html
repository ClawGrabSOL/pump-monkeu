Millbrook Road Bridge Repairs to Begin Monday Morning, Detour in Effect Through Late May

The state transportation cabinet will close a half-mile section of Millbrook Road beginning Monday at 6 a.m. to allow structural repairs on a bridge that has been flagged since last summer's inspection.
By Dale Epperson · Staff Reporter
Published April 6, 2025 · 6:30 a.m.
🌉
The Millbrook Road bridge over Short Fork Creek will be closed to through traffic beginning Monday, April 7, while a KYTC contractor repairs deteriorating deck planking and two support members. Harlan County Courier / file

HARLAN COUNTY — Drivers who regularly use Millbrook Road should plan on an alternate route starting Monday morning, April 7, when the Kentucky Transportation Cabinet will close the bridge over Short Fork Creek for structural repairs expected to last through the third week of May.

The bridge, which was flagged during a routine state inspection last July, has two deteriorating support members and deck planking that inspectors rated as functionally deficient. The structure has remained passable for passenger vehicles since the inspection, but KYTC officials say the repairs cannot be deferred into a second season without risking a downgrade in load rating that would require immediate closure regardless.

The designated detour routes drivers south on Millbrook Road to Highway 119, east to the Evarts connector, and back north on Clear Fork Road — adding approximately eight minutes to most trips, according to KYTC estimates. Residents along the closed section with driveways on the north end of the bridge will have access maintained from the north approach at all times.

"We know this is an inconvenience and we don't take that lightly. The goal is to have the road fully open before the end of May, and we're going to hold that contractor to that schedule." — District 11 Transportation Cabinet spokesperson

Harlan County Road Department Director Tim Feltner said the county has coordinated with KYTC on signage and will have staff on site Monday morning to assist with any traffic flow issues during the first few days of the closure. School bus routes in the affected area have already been adjusted by the county transportation department.

Construction is scheduled to begin at 6:00 a.m. Monday, April 7, weather permitting. Updates can be found on the KYTC District 11 website or by calling (606) 573-4440.
