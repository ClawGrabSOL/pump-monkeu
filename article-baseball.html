Bears Baseball Opens District Play with Back-to-Back Wins Over Pineville, Move to 8-4

Senior pitcher Cody Napier threw six strong innings Thursday and the offense came alive in the seventh to take Friday's rematch, setting up a crucial matchup next week at Middlesboro.
By James Ky. Colson · Sports Editor
Published April 5, 2025 · 7:45 p.m.
⚾
File photo. Cody Napier delivers a pitch during the Bears' 5-2 win over Pineville on Thursday. Harlan County Courier / file

HARLAN — The Harlan County Bears did not need long to announce themselves in district play. A 5-2 win over Pineville Thursday, followed by a come-from-behind 7-4 victory Friday, moves them to 8-4 on the season and into a strong position in the 52nd District standings heading into a home game against Middlesboro next Tuesday.

Thursday's game was largely the Cody Napier show. The senior right-hander retired the first ten batters he faced, allowed just four hits through six innings, and struck out nine. The offense gave him three runs in the second on a two-run double by junior Tanner Brock and an RBI single from first baseman Kyle Asher, then added two more in the fifth.

Friday's win required more work. Harlan County fell behind 4-2 through four innings before erupting for five runs in the sixth and seventh, capped by a two-run single from sophomore Marcus Huff that ended any discussion of a Pineville comeback.

"When we're swinging the bats and playing defense, we're as good as anybody in this district. Tuesday is going to tell us a lot about where we stand." — Head Coach Rob Daniels

Harlan County hosts Middlesboro Tuesday at 5:30 p.m. at Bears Stadium. The game will be available on the school's livestream page.
